Projects

Ryan elevates architectural craft by using computational methodologies to realize complex design visions.

On the Madera Cyber Innovation Center at the Air Force Academy, he rationalized a complex self-supporting spiral staircase and custom curved glass guardrails, delivering a turnkey digital-to-physical workflow that preserved design integrity and contributed to its 2025 ASPIRE Honor Award.

Similarly, for the Wake Tech Parking Deck, Ryan leveraged parametric scripts to optimize a 350-foot reflective facade. When budget cuts threatened the design, he algorithmically redesigned the panel layout within days, maintaining the aesthetic intent and securing a 2019 AIA South Atlantic Region Honor Award.

Practice Graphic

Practice

Ryan advances architectural practice by developing scalable frameworks that shift emerging technology from ad-hoc use to firm-wide standard operating procedures.

During the James B. Hunt Jr. Library project, he developed a custom Construction Administration database so successful it was deployed across all firm projects. At Fitts-Woolard Hall, he built database tools to validate programming compliance and visually track punch-lists in real-time.

Today, as Practice Technology Leader, he leads Clark Nexsen's AI Governance. He established a national-model AI Strategy and ethical guidelines, creating firm-wide 'AI Toolkits' that safely integrate generative design tools while managing liability and protecting professional standards.

Industry Graphic

Industry

Ryan drives industry-wide advancement by bridging the digital divide, translating complex computation into accessible strategies for the broader profession.

As the National Chair of the AIA Technology in Architectural Practice (TAP) community, he steered national technology dialogue and curated the Building Connections Congress, pioneering livestreaming and publishing permanent educational resources on the AIAU platform.

He represents the AIA on national boards and juries, including serving as Chair of the National AIA Innovation Awards. Through university lectures and published articles, Ryan demystifies advanced workflows, sharing his research openly to elevate professional standards and secure the architect's relevance in a digital future.
