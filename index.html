Wake Tech : Parking Deck 2

2008-2009

850 space Parking Deck

Architect | Facade Detailing, Grasshopper Scripting, Revit Architecture Lead

The Wake Tech campus is split by a natural wetland and forest. The site for the deck is located directly on one edge of the forest. All student commute to campus requiring ample parking. The result of this was a deck that is proportionally long and used frequently. To break up the monotony of the long edge, a parametrically generated stainless steel facade was designed. The panels respond to the street edge by being closely spaced, and the wetland edge by opening up as the turn the corner. Both sides of the deck are visible by students, one side is visible from the street, and the other by a pedestrian bridge that moves across the wetlands and connects to the main library of the campus.

Duke University: Edens Quad Dorm Renovation

2014-2015

154,000 sqft Dorm Renovation

Architect

Duke university wanted to increase the desirability of the Edens Quad dorms. Students simply didn't want to stay in the closed off Brutilist quad that turned its back on the rest of campus. The University also wanted the dorms to feel more open, inviting and connected to the rest of the campus. The big idea was to gather up all the small shared commons spaces that were spread throughout the dorms and collect them into one place (infilling them with dorm rooms). The final idea included cutting a hole through one of the buildings to act as the gateway to the quad, making it more inviting. It also was the location where the new study commons and gaming room was relocated. Other parts of the renovation included reworking the outdoor spaces to be more inviting and usable, renovation of each dorms commons rooms by removing the Brutalist concrete panels and replacing them with glass, and the addition of amenities such as a Gym.

City of Raleigh: Critical Public Safety Center

2014-2015

Architect. Facade Detailing, and Coordination.

This project is the location for emergency services for the City of Raleigh. It is in operation 24-7 and also is the location where the city is operated in times of emergency. Unique elements are that the building is in compliance with DOD blast and bulletproof requirements. Our firm was responsible for the design of the Exterior Skin, and the initial layout concepts of the building. I was responsible for the thermally broken brick lintel detail that involved pulling the lintel off the facade, so that the insulation could be continuous behind the steel. A company first.

CANstruction

I created a Grasshopper script to rapidly visualize and iterate the design for the CANStruction fund raising event. We partnered with RedHat, a tech company, so I thought that we should try to implement as much technology as possible. The script allowed for a form and can heights to be input and then it would generate a visualization of the final result including can count. After the design was completed and sent to the CNC fabricator, the quote was too high because it was slightly wider than the material provided. A quick reduction in the size of the underlaying form generated a new output that used 21 less sheets and saved over $1000 in labor. Without parametric scripting the entire drawings would have had to be redone.
